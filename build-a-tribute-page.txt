    <h2>This is his story</h2>
    <p>Credit: <a href="https://kinginstitute.stanford.edu/king-resources/major-king-events-chronology-1929-1968">Stanford University</a></p>

    <ul>
      <li>1929: Martin Luther King Jr. is born in Atlanta, Georgia.</li>
      <li>1944: Enrolls at Morehouse College at age 15, skipping 9th and 12th grades.</li>
      <li>1948: Graduates from Morehouse College with a Bachelor’s degree in sociology. Enrolls at Crozer Theological Seminary in Pennsylvania.</li>
      <li>1951: Graduates from Crozer Theological Seminary with a Bachelor of Divinity degree and begins doctoral studies at Boston University.</li>
      <li>1953: Marries Coretta Scott in Marion, Alabama.</li>
      <li>1955: Receives his Ph.D. in Systematic Theology from Boston University Rosa Parks is arrested, sparking the Montgomery Bus Boycott. King is elected president of the Montgomery Improvement Association, launching the boycott.</li>
      <li>1956: Montgomery Bus Boycott ends after 381 days following a Supreme Court ruling that segregation on public buses is unconstitutional.</li>
      <li>1963: Writes the “Letter from Birmingham Jail” after being arrested during a nonviolent protest in Birmingham, Alabama Delivers his famous “I Have a Dream” speech at the March on Washington for Jobs and Freedom.</li>
      <li>1964: The Civil Rights Act is signed into law. Receives the Nobel Peace Prize for his work in combating racial inequality through nonviolent resistance.</li>
      <li>1965: Leads the Selma to Montgomery marches, pushing for African American voting rights. The Voting Rights Act is signed into law.</li>
      <li>1968: Martin Luther King Jr. is assassinated in Memphis, Tennessee, while supporting a sanitation workers' strike.</li>
    </ul>
